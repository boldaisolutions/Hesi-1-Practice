🚪 Membrane Transport

✅ Passive (NO ATP)

  • Diffusion — high → low concentration
  • Osmosis — water toward higher solute
  • Filtration — pressure-driven movement
  • Facilitated diffusion — channel-assisted

⚡ Active (USES ATP)

  • Na⁺/K⁺ pump — 3 Na out, 2 K in
  • Endocytosis — cell engulfs material
  • Exocytosis — cell expels material
  • Phagocytosis — engulfs bacteria/particles
  • Pinocytosis — engulfs fluids
Water follows SALT during osmosis. Water moves toward the area of HIGHER solute concentration. "Water follows salt" = #1 HESI fluid balance principle.

⚡ Action Potential / Resting Membrane

⚡ ATP Depletion — Injury Sequence
↓ Oxygen→ ↓ ATP→ Na/K pump fails→ Na⁺ floods in→ Cell SWELLS (1st sign)→ Cell death
🚨 HESI ALERT: Cellular SWELLING is the FIRST and EARLIEST sign of reversible cell injury. When ATP drops → Na⁺/K⁺ pump fails → Na⁺ accumulates inside → water follows = swelling. This is tested repeatedly.
💀 Necrosis vs. Apoptosis

Necrosis (Messy)

  • Unplanned — caused by injury
  • Causes INFLAMMATION
  • Cell swells then bursts
  • Releases cell contents
  • Types: coagulative, liquefactive, caseous

Apoptosis (Controlled)

  • Programmed — orderly
  • NO inflammation
  • Controlled by p53 gene
  • Normal development & homeostasis
  • Cell shrinks, not bursts
Necrosis = Nasty & messy. Apoptosis = Autopilot programmed death (orderly, no mess). Gout = URATE crystal deposition causing pain. Dystrophic calcification = calcium in injured tissue.

🧬 Polygenic vs. Multifactorial

Polygenic

  • Multiple genes only
  • Bell curve distribution
  • Examples: height, skin color, blood pressure

Multifactorial

  • Multiple genes + ENVIRONMENT
  • Most common chronic diseases
  • Examples: DM, HTN, heart disease, obesity, schizophrenia
